Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our therapy services and approach

What conditions do you specialize in treating?

+

We specialize in a wide range of neurological conditions including autism spectrum disorder, ADHD, developmental delays, learning disabilities, motor disorders, cerebral palsy, and stroke recovery in children. Our team of experienced therapists is trained to address various neurological challenges with evidence-based treatment approaches.

What ages of children do you serve?

+

We serve children from infancy through adolescence (typically ages 0-18). Early intervention is crucial in neurological therapy, and our team is experienced in working with children of all developmental stages. We tailor our approach based on your child's age, developmental level, and specific needs.

Do you offer both individual and group therapy?

+

Yes, we offer both individual and group therapy options depending on your child's needs and goals. Individual sessions allow for personalized, one-on-one attention, while group therapy provides peer interaction opportunities and social skill development. Our therapists will recommend the best approach during your initial consultation.

How long does a typical therapy session last?

+

Standard therapy sessions are 45-60 minutes, depending on your child's age, attention span, and treatment plan. We also offer extended sessions of 90 minutes for comprehensive assessments or intensive therapy programs. The duration is customized based on your child's needs and therapeutic goals.

How often should my child attend therapy sessions?

+

Frequency depends on your child's condition severity and treatment goals. Most children benefit from 1-3 sessions per week. Intensive programs may require more frequent sessions, while maintenance therapy might be less frequent. Our therapists will recommend an optimal schedule during the initial assessment and adjust as needed based on progress.

What should we expect during the first appointment?

+

Your first appointment includes:

  • Comprehensive assessment of your child's condition and needs
  • Review of medical history and previous treatments
  • Goal-setting discussion with parents/guardians
  • Introduction to our therapy approach and environment
  • Personalized treatment plan development
Allow 90 minutes for this initial consultation.

What is the cost of therapy sessions?

+

Session costs vary depending on the type of therapy and therapist experience level. Individual sessions range from $75-$150 per hour, while group sessions are more affordable. We offer package discounts for longer-term commitments. Please contact us for detailed pricing information tailored to your specific needs.

Do you accept insurance?

+

Yes, we work with most major insurance providers. Many therapy services are covered under your insurance plan with a valid prescription or referral from your child's doctor. We recommend contacting your insurance provider to verify coverage before scheduling. Our billing team can assist with insurance inquiries and documentation.

Is financial assistance available?

+

We believe every child deserves access to quality therapy. We offer sliding scale fees based on family income, payment plans to help manage costs, and partnerships with community organizations that may provide financial assistance. Please discuss your financial situation with our team during registration.

How do I schedule an appointment?

+

Scheduling is easy and flexible. You can:

  • Call us at (555) 123-4567 during business hours
  • Complete our online scheduling form on the website
  • Email us at appointments@neurocare.com
  • Request a consultation through our contact page
We typically have availability within 1-2 weeks of your request.

Can therapy be conducted virtually?

+

We offer virtual therapy sessions for select therapy types and conditions. Teletherapy works well for consultation, follow-up sessions, and certain treatment approaches. However, some therapies (particularly those requiring hands-on guidance) are more effective in-person. Our team will discuss which format is best for your child's specific needs.

What if my child has additional questions during treatment?

+

Our therapists encourage ongoing communication and are available to address questions and concerns. We provide regular progress updates, hold monthly family conferences to discuss goals and progress, and have an open-door policy for questions. You can reach out to your therapist between sessions if urgent concerns arise.
